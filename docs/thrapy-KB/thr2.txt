
## What This Research Covers

### 🌍 Global Scale of the Crisis
More than **1 billion people** worldwide live with mental health disorders, yet only 2% of health budgets address them[1]. In the U.S., 23.4% of adults — over 61 million — experienced mental illness in 2024[2][3]. The treatment gap is enormous: fewer than 10% of people in low-income countries receive any care[1].

### 🧠 Trauma Neuroscience & Polyvagal Theory
The report details how trauma rewires the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex[25][26], and includes the full 2025-updated Polyvagal Theory framework for clinical application[27][28].

### ⚠️ AI Safety Architecture (Critical for Your Build)
The **ASL-MH framework** (5 safety levels for mental health AI)[29], plus key findings: most existing chatbots **fail to refer users to professionals in crisis**[30], and reliability decreases as severity increases[31]. The report includes explicit lists of what AI **can** and **cannot** do, mandatory crisis escalation protocols, and the **AI-TIC trauma-informed AI framework**[32][33].

### 🕸️ Knowledge Graph Design
Includes node schema (Disorder, Symptom, Trauma, Treatment, AssessmentTool, CrisisProtocol, EthicsConstraint), edge/relationship types, recommended standards (SNOMED-CT, ICD-11, DSM-5-TR, LOINC)[34][35], and the **Mental Disorders Knowledge Graph (MDKG)** with 10M+ relations from a 2025 *Nature Communications* paper[36].
