## What This Knowledge Base Covers

The report is organized in **7 parts** built specifically for your voice interview + NPC narrative game architecture:

### 🧠 Why It Works (Part I)
Your core mechanics are validated. **SPARX** — a CBT-embedded RPG from New Zealand — was shown in a BMJ randomized controlled trial to be *non-inferior to face-to-face therapy* for adolescent depression[1], with effects lasting 3+ months[2]. A 2025 APA *Monitor* article confirmed TTRPGs show clinical promise for anxiety, depression, trauma, and ADHD[3]. Avatar-based therapy research confirms anonymity and self-objectification through characters **increases self-disclosure** and reduces barriers to engagement[4].

### 📢 Universal Rules (Part II)
- The **OARS framework** (Open questions, Affirmations, Reflective listening, Summarizing) is your voice interview's conversational engine[5][6]
- At least **70% of questions must be open-ended**[7]
- Never ask **"Why?"** — it's clinically proven to generate defensiveness[8]
- A full table of what to never say across all disorders with validated replacements[9][10][11]

### 🗂️ All 10 Disorders — Structured Tables (Part III)
Each disorder gets:
- **What opens them up** (communication approach)
- **Exact phrases that help** (copy-paste ready)
- **Hard avoidance rules** (what will close them down or cause harm)
- **NPC design considerations** specific to that disorder's psychology

### 🎙️ Voice Interview Science (Part IV)
- Silence is therapeutic — never fill pauses[12][13]
- **Observer-perspective questions** (asking what a character would think/feel) create the therapeutic distance that makes difficult material safe[14]
- The **Graduated Disclosure Architecture (GDA)** — a 4-layer model for how deeply the interview should go, gated by safety and therapist review

### 🎮 Game + NPC Design (Part V)
- A complete **NPC Archetype Map** (The Companion, Mentor, Mirror, Peer in Recovery, etc.) mapped to therapeutic roles and disorder fits[15][3]
- The **Projective Identification** framework — how users unconsciously project their inner world onto NPCs, and how to design for it[16]
- **Gamification Mechanics Table** — 12 mechanics each mapped to the clinical mechanism they activate and the disorders they fit best[17][18]

### 🛡️ Safety Architecture (Part VI)
Hard trigger rules, therapist-guided session visibility requirements, and the exact scope-communication script the voice assistant should deliver to users.
